The Strategy of Prioritization#

Once you’ve mapped your terrain, prioritize your objectives. Generals prioritize battles they can decisively win and ones that align with their larger strategy. Here’s how to apply that mindset:

The Eisenhower Matrix#

Divide your tasks into four quadrants:

  1. Urgent and Important: Tasks requiring immediate attention.
  2. Important but Not Urgent: Long-term goals; schedule these in advance.
  3. Urgent but Not Important: Delegate these tasks if possible.
  4. Neither Urgent nor Important: Minimize these as they are distractions.

Pareto Principle#

Remember, 80% of your results often come from 20% of your efforts. Identify those critical tasks that deliver maximum outcome and focus on them.
