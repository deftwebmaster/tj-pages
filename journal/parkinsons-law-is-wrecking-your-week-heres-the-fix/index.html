What is Parkinson’s Law?#

At its core, Parkinson’s Law is a principle stating that “work expands to fill the time available for its completion.” Coined by Cyril Northcote Parkinson in an essay published in The Economist in 1955, the law humorously sheds light on how people tend to procrastinate when more time is allocated to a task than necessary.

Why Does Parkinson’s Law Wreak Havoc?#

  • Procrastination: When deadlines are distant, it’s easy to delay starting the work. Procrastination sets in, leading to a rushed completion phase.
  • Lack of Focus: More time often leads to loss of focus. Without urgency, it’s easy to get distracted.
  • Low Productivity: Extended timelines encourage idle behavior, reducing the overall productivity and efficiency of work.
